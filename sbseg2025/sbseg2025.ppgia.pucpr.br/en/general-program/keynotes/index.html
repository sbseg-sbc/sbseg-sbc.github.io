Keynote Speakers

Anderson C. A. Nascimento

Title: Quantum Computing: Recent Advancements and Their Impact on Cybersecurity

Abstract: Quantum computing is rapidly transitioning from theory to practice. Breakthroughs in stable qubits, high-fidelity quantum gates, improved error-correction techniques, and the growing accessibility of cloud-based quantum platforms are reshaping the field. With sustained industry investment, the question of building fault-tolerant quantum computers is no longer if but when.

In this keynote, I will survey the latest developments in quantum computing and explore their profound implications for cybersecurity. We will examine both the challenges and opportunities these advancements present, highlighting what security practitioners and researchers must understand today to prepare for the post-quantum era.

Data: 03 de Setembro (Quarta-feira) das 11:00 às 12:00
Sala: Auditório Pacífico

Christian Doerr, University of Potsdam

Title: Towards intelligence-driven cybersecurity

Abstract: Despite decades of investment in cybersecurity technologies and frameworks, the frequency, scale, and impact of cyberattacks continue to escalate. Today, the majority of organizations report that they have experienced a cyber attack within the last 12 months, and nearly half of organizations fear going out of business as a result of an attack. This paradox raises fundamental questions about the effectiveness of prevailing security paradigms and the assumptions that underpin them. Much of today’s defensive posture relies on outdated threat models, assumed attack vectors, and limited insights into real attacker behavior, instead of empirical insights. In this talk, we advocate for a shift towards intelligence-driven cybersecurity—an approach grounded in empirical insight into how adversaries operate, how attacks unfold, and how defenders can respond more effectively. Drawing on findings from threat intelligence, attack forensics, and behavioral research, we argue for a more scientific and adaptive approach to defense design—one that moves beyond reactive measures to anticipate and disrupt adversarial operations. This perspective calls for rethinking core security principles, embracing data-driven methodologies, and closing the gap between academic research and operational practice.

Biography: Christian Doerr (Member, IEEE) received a joint Ph.D. degree in computer science and cognitive science from the University of Colorado at Boulder, USA. He is a Professor of Cyber Security and Enterprise Security and the Director of the Cyber Threat Intelligence Lab, Hasso Plattner Institute, Potsdam, Germany. His research focuses on network security, cyber threat intelligence, and situational awareness.

Data: 03 de Setembro (Quarta-feira) das 16:00 às 17:00

David Mohaisen, University of Central Florida

Title: AI in Cybersecurity: Attribution, Inference, and the Dynamics of the Attack?Defense Arms Race

Abstract: The rapid progress of artificial intelligence has ushered in new methodologies for modeling, learning, and inference across domains. In security, this evolution offers both promise and peril. This keynote explores the dual role of AI in the cybersecurity landscape–empowering defenders with advanced capabilities such as code authorship attribution, LLM-driven vulnerability description enhancement, automated repair, and in-network intelligence for self-driving networks, while also exposing novel vulnerabilities, including privacy breaches in emerging modalities such as AR/VR systems. Drawing from empirical studies and system-level research, we illustrate how AI reshapes the balance in the attack-defense arms race. The talk concludes with a discussion of open research questions and methodological challenges, calling for a deeper synthesis of AI robustness, interpretability, and trust in the design of future-secure systems.

Biography: David Mohaisen is a Professor of Computer Science at the University of Central Florida, where he directs the Security and Analytics Lab (SEAL). His research spans systems and security, with contributions in malware analysis, adversarial machine learning, privacy inference, blockchain security, in-network intelligence, and trusted hardware. His work appears in top venues such as IEEE S&P, USENIX Security, CCS, NDSS, HPCA, CHI, VR, and ICCV. Prior to UCF, he held research and faculty positions in both academia and industry, including at Verisign Labs, the University at Buffalo (SUNY), and ETRI in South Korea. He has led major research efforts funded by NSF, NRF, AFRL, and various industry partners. He has served on editorial boards for IEEE TDSC, TMC, TPDS, and TCC, and regularly contributes to the organization of leading conferences, including IEEE S&P, CCS, NDSS, etc. He is a senior member of ACM and IEEE, and a Distinguished Speaker (ACM) and Distinguished Visitor (IEEE).

Guofei Gu, Texas A&M University

Title: Towards AI-powered Proactive Cyber Defense

Abstract: Most current cyber defense solutions remain passive and reactive, primarily designed to detect and respond to known threats after they occur. However, as cyber attacks escalate in both frequency and severity—resulting in increasingly devastating impacts—this traditional approach proves insufficient. The rapidly evolving economic incentives behind profit-driven cybercrime continue to shift the security landscape in attackers’ favor, deepening the fundamental asymmetries between attackers and defenders. Clearly, passive defenses alone cannot effectively counteract these dynamic threats. Instead, proactive strategies that anticipate attacks and disrupt them before damage occurs are urgently needed. This talk emphasizes proactive cyber defense approaches aimed at staying ahead of attackers rather than perpetually following them. We will present case studies of new proactive cybersecurity techniques developed at the SUCCESS Lab and discuss what role of AI can play in advancing proactive cybersecurity.

Biography:  Dr. Guofei Gu is a professor and holder of the Eppright Professorship in Engineering in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at Texas A&M University (TAMU). Before coming to TAMU, he received his Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from the College of Computing, Georgia Institute of Technology. His research interests are in network and systems security.

Dr. Gu is an IEEE Fellow and an ACM Distinguished Member. He is a recipient of 2010 NSF CAREER Award, 2013 AFOSR Young Investigator Award, and Texas A&M Presidential Impact Fellow, among several others. He has pioneered several new research directions such as botnet detection and software-defined network (SDN) security, and his research received 2010 IEEE S&P Best Student Paper Award, 2015 ICDCS Best Paper Award, 2022 ASIACCS Best Paper Award, and 2023 ACSAC Test of Time Award. He is currently directing the SUCCESS Lab at TAMU.

Matheus Domingos

Título: Data Science no contexto de Prevenção de Fraudes: Impactos, Desafios e Perspectivas

Resumo: A Unico é uma empresa brasileira líder em identidade digital, unicórnio avaliado em mais de USD 2.6 bilhões e reconhecida como uma das mais inovadoras da América Latina. Presente em mais de 800 empresas, incluindo os maiores bancos e fintechs do país, desenvolvemos soluções que reconhecem 9 a cada 10 brasileiros com 100% de certeza.

Nesta palestra apresentamos como o time de IDLive utiliza ciência de dados de forma estratégica para combater fraudes em verificações de prova de vida (liveness). Nossa abordagem combina técnicas avançadas de machine learning, visão computacional e análise de dados para detectar e investigar tentativas de fraude em tempo real. Serão apresentados quatro projetos principais: Modelo de Similaridade de Imagens usando redes neurais convolucionais e transfer learning; Análise de Séries Temporais com algoritmos de grafos para detecção de padrões comportamentais; Análise de Séries Curtas com Visão Computacional utilizando hash perceptual e clustering não-supervisionado; e Análise de Propagação Geoespacial para investigação por proximidade espacial.

O diferencial da Unico está na arquitetura “Prova de Vida 4×4” – única solução no mercado com 4 camadas de segurança e 4 camadas de reação que operam em paralelo, permitindo coletar dados do fraudador sem revelar nossos mecanismos de defesa. Os resultados demonstram efetividade superior: 9x mais efetiva em detecção de injeções, recall de 96% em ataques conhecidos, e capacidade de reagir 4x mais rápido a novas fraudes. Na palestra abordaremos também os desafios técnicos enfrentados, como custo computacional para processar milhões de transações diárias, adaptação constante às evoluções dos fraudadores, e balanceamento entre precisão e recall. Serão discutidas também as perspectivas futuras, incluindo bloqueio online em tempo real e evolução para aprendizado não-supervisionado.

Short Bio: Panther é Senior Engineering Manager na Unico, com mais de 12 anos de experiência em tecnologia. Formado em Ciências da Computação pela UEL (Londrina) e pós-graduado em Sistemas Móveis, hoje lidera a área de Interfaces, responsável por todas as APIs de integração da Unico. Ao longo da carreira, Panther atuou como Staff Software Engineer e Tech Lead, sendo peça-chave na criação dos SDKs móveis da Unico — atualmente o segundo maior da América Latina, em expansão global e utilizado diariamente por dezenas de milhões de usuários.
